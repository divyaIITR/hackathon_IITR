About IITR

IITR is big NOT as big as it might be thought.But it's big enough to deter our from attending boring lectures at 8am.Nay, lectures in general.Along with other benefits,we are assured that campus is always more happening than the city out-side.Well, probably because there is nocity outside.But, no worries, big cities are merely filled with dance and fun-things that no college student would want anyway.The institute finds its roots way back in 1845.It was the time when speaking in a British accent did not make all of us effeminate and top hats were very much in fashion, so much so that people dressed up in 3-piece suits just to go have dinner in the Bhawan messes(where they were served in silverware by turbaned waiters).In 1847, the institute was officially established.It was renamed as the Thomson College of Civil Engineering in 1854 in honour of its founder,Sir James Thomson , lieutenant governor of the Ganges canal.

IIT Roorkee To Organise 5-Day Virtual Entrepreneurship Summit

Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Roorkee will organise a five-day annual virtual entrepreneurship summit (E-Summit) from January 10 to host discussions with famous entrepreneurs. The E-summit will have various events including Ideastorm - the B-Plan competition, Productathon- a software hackathon and Sale-Able- a sales competition.This year due to the on-going COVID-19 pandemic, the E-summit is being held virtually .More than 10,000 students, 100 startups,50 speakers and 20 venture capitalists from across regions in Asia are expected to participate in the summit. For further details, people can visit the IIT Roorkee E summit official website esummit.in

CAMPUS LIFE

All freshmen reside in Rajendra bhawan and fresh-girls in Sarojini Bhawan. RJB residents with a JEE Rank under 2000 are allotted single rooms which means they get to spend the first year without all the snags that come with having a troublesome roommate. Bhawans come with most of the basic amenities one may need in day-to-day life.

• A bathroom that you share with the 50 people that live in the same row as you, along with a dog or two if you’re lucky.
• A mess that serves… food…
• A laundry guy that knocks on your door twice a week and can be counted on as a reliable alarm. There’s also a laundry room that requires you to provide your own detergent but is quite a popular alternative among people who find the laundry guy’s early morning timings to be inconvenient.
• A number of open spaces in case you ever feel like catching fresh air or are unfortunate enough to be the victim of a GPL too grand to be contained within the hostel corridors.
• A Gym for evening work-out sessions, whatever your reasons for that may be.
• There’s also ungodly fast LAN connection in every room, perfect for multiplayer showdowns and movie nights arranged on a whim.
• A stationery shop - your life saviour for all the last-minute graphs and practical _les that you’ll need.
